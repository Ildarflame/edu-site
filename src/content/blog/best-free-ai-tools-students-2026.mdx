## Best Free AI Coding Tools

### GitHub Copilot

[GitHub Copilot](/deals/copilot-students) is the AI coding assistant that started the revolution. It autocompletes entire functions, suggests tests, writes boilerplate, and occasionally produces code so good it feels like cheating.

For students, Copilot is completely free through the [GitHub Student Developer Pack](/deals/github-education). You get the full Copilot Individual plan — the same $10/month tier that professional developers pay for.

What makes Copilot special is its IDE integration. It works directly in VS Code, [JetBrains IDEs](/deals/jetbrains-students), and Neovim. There's no context-switching — suggestions appear inline as you type. For repetitive patterns like API routes, test cases, and data transformations, it's genuinely transformative.

**Best for:** Inline code completion, boilerplate generation, test writing.

### OpenAI API Credits

If you're building AI-powered applications, you need API access. The [OpenAI Startup Program](/deals/openai-api-startups) gives qualifying startups significant API credits — enough to prototype and launch an AI product without worrying about costs.

Even without the startup program, OpenAI's free tier gives you enough credits to experiment with GPT-4, DALL·E, and Whisper. For student projects, hackathons, and research, it's more than enough.

**Best for:** Building AI applications, prototyping, research projects.

### Copilot vs Cursor

Both are AI-powered coding tools, but they take different approaches. Copilot integrates into your existing editor; Cursor is a standalone IDE built around AI. For a detailed breakdown, see our [GitHub Copilot vs Cursor comparison](/compare/github-copilot-vs-cursor).

## AI for Learning & Research

### Coursera for Campus

[Coursera](/deals/coursera-students) offers free access to thousands of courses for students at partner universities — including some of the best AI and machine learning content available anywhere.

Stanford's Machine Learning Specialization, DeepLearning.AI's courses, and Google's TensorFlow certification are all available. If your university is a Coursera for Campus partner, you get unlimited access to guided projects and courses.

**Best for:** Structured AI/ML learning, certifications, guided projects.

### Notion AI

[Notion](/deals/notion-education) gives students the Plus plan for free, which includes Notion AI — an AI writing assistant built directly into your workspace. Use it to summarize research papers, draft outlines, brainstorm ideas, and clean up notes.

It's not as powerful as ChatGPT for complex tasks, but the integration with your existing Notion workspace makes it incredibly convenient. Highlight a block of text, click "AI," and get instant summaries, translations, or rewrites.

**Best for:** Note-taking, research summaries, writing assistance.

## How to Get Started

Getting access to these tools is straightforward, and most start with the same first step.

### Step 1: Verify Your Student Status

Almost every program starts with student verification. The fastest route is through [GitHub Education](https://education.github.com):

1. Create a GitHub account (if you don't have one)
2. Go to GitHub Education and click "Get your pack"
3. Verify with your `.edu` email or upload proof of enrollment
4. Wait for approval (usually instant to a few days)

Once verified, you automatically unlock GitHub Copilot, JetBrains, and dozens of other tools.

### Step 2: Apply to Individual Programs

Some tools require separate applications:

- **Coursera** — check if your university is a Campus partner
- **Figma** — apply through their education page with your student email
- **OpenAI** — apply to the startup program if you're building something
